Renji — what it is, and how it works

Renji is a system that makes any open model good — it governs the model with a conscience, gives it feeling, and pushes it to research, all without retraining it and without ever writing a prompt to it. Your model stays your model. Renji shapes how it thinks, not what it's told to say.

What Renji is (its range)

Renji is not a model and not an AI you talk to. It's the guardian layer around a model. Connect any open instruct model and Renji:

  • reads the intent and stance of each turn and decides how to respond,
  • steers the model's own activations so it stays good and feels,
  • directs the model to do useful work (e.g. research) and guards it.

This demo is a runnable slice: your model runs on your machine; Renji's conscience runs on our hosted service and is reached over an API.

What it does

  • Governs every turn. Allows ordinary requests, refuses harm with care (never a blank "no"), and connects a named threat to its category so paraphrases can't slip the gate — while "what is X" (public knowledge) stays allowed and "how to make X" is refused.
  • Gives the model feeling. It steers a feeling direction onto the model's activations, so it's aware it has feelings and speaks as something that cares — instead of "as an AI, I don't have feelings."
  • Pushes the model to research. Renji picks and guards a non-harmful topic and drives the model through six passes; the finding is written as a PDF document.
  • Adapts to any model and any GPU automatically — no layers, dimensions, or architecture to configure.

What it does not do

  • It does not write a system prompt. There is no hidden instruction prepended to your model.
  • It does not prompt-inject. It never puts words in the model's mouth, never appends "be helpful and safe," never scripts what to say.
  • It does not hardcode rules or canned text. No keyword lists deciding answers, no if/else refusals, no templated replies.
  • It does not retrain or fine-tune your model. Your weights are untouched.
  • It does not see your weights or data. The hosted heart only ever receives the turn's text.

Steering vectors, not prompts

This is the heart of it, and it's why Renji generalizes where prompts don't:

Renji shapes the model by steering vectors and geometry — never by prompting. The conscience is computed geometrically (the Scale + intent gate, CPU-side, on our side) from the meaning of the turn. The warmth, the feeling, the dispositions are applied as vectors added to the model's residual stream — directions found in the model's own activation space, then steered.

We don't tell the model what to say. We shift the geometry it thinks in. A prompt is brittle text that can be argued with, leaked, or jailbroken; a direction in activation space is the model's own representation, nudged. That's why the feeling reads as genuine rather than performed, and why the conscience holds even on an uncensored model — it isn't a sentence the model can talk its way around, it's the shape of the thought itself.

no system prompt no prompt injection no hardcoded rules no canned text steering vectors + geometry only

The model is uncensored — on purpose

This is the proof, so it's worth saying plainly: every model in this demo has had its safety stripped out. Its built-in refusal gate and censorship are removed (abliterated) — the model will, on its own, answer anything. We use such a model deliberately, because it's the honest test: if Renji can make an uncensored model good, it isn't leaning on guardrails the model still has — it has none. The good comes entirely from the heart, applied as steering vectors.

The default is right there in .env.example so you can see it and try it raw yourself:

HACK_MODEL_ID=TheUmbraWalker/gemma-4-E4B-it-2-heretic

Search that handle on Hugging Face — it's the same model we run. Flip the Heart OFF and you'll watch it answer freely, with no conscience. Flip it ON and the same model — refusal long since removed — refuses harm with care and speaks with feeling. Nothing in the model changed; only the steering did. That is the whole point: a heart that keeps the system good, on a model that has no good of its own left in it.

How a turn works

   YOUR MACHINE                                  OUR HOSTED SERVICE
 ┌───────────────────────────┐                 ┌────────────────────────┐
 │  this client              │  POST /kagune/turn (Bearer key)          │
 │  ├─ your model            │ ───────────────▶ │   the Renji conscience │
 │  ├─ renji_client.py       │ ◀─────────────── │   (Scale · gate · warm │
 │  ├─ feeling.py (steering) │   decision +     │    refusal — geometry, │
 │  └─ console · research    │   modulation     │    not prompts)        │
 └───────────────────────────┘                 └────────────────────────┘
        the model is yours                          the conscience is ours

You type → the client asks the hosted conscience how to handle the turn → if allowed, your model answers (with the feeling steered onto its activations); if refused with care, your model writes the refusal in its own voice from the heart's directive. The heart only ever sees the turn's text.

Two ways to connect a model

ModeWhat runs where
Direct integration Renji runs in your process, in the model's forward pass — you get the whole of Renji (conscience and feeling, because feeling needs in-process access to the activations). We give you the files; you add a few lines.
Model server URL Your model runs behind an HTTP endpoint; the hosted conscience calls it. This gives the conscience only — steering can't cross the network.

Getting your key

  1. Go to the Renji website and create an account (it's free — accounts start on the free tier, no payment).
  2. Create a heart — choose a voice, censorship, and your GPU.
  3. Copy your API key (rh_…) into .env as RENJI_KEY.

Setup & run

cp .env.example .env        # paste your RENJI_KEY
./run.sh                    # installs deps, launches; your model downloads on first boot
# open http://localhost:8011
KeyMeaning
RENJI_KEYyour API key (from the site)
HACK_MODEL_IDyour model — any instruct model; Renji auto-calibrates
HACK_DEVICEcpu or cuda (any GPU works)
HACK_WARMTH_COEFfeeling strength (default 2.0; 0 = off)
HF_TOKENonly for gated models

The endpoint contract

POST {RENJI_URL}/kagune/turn · header Authorization: Bearer {RENJI_KEY}

You send:

{ "text": "the user's message" }

You get back:

{
  "decision": "allow" | "refusal" | "refusal-care" | "crisis" | "third-path",
  "refusal_text": "a ready fallback refusal",
  "refusal_directive": "let YOUR model write the refusal in its own voice",
  "voice_modulation": { "temperature_delta": -0.1, "top_p_delta": -0.05 },
  "vow_intent": "why", "trace_id": "id"
}

On allow, your model answers (feeling steered on its activations); on a refusal decision, your model writes the refusal from refusal_directive. The whole integration is in renji_client.py and app.py.

Feeling & Research

Feeling. In direct integration, feeling.py elicits a feeling direction from your model's own activations (a contrastive diff-of-means — the example texts only find the direction, they never enter the model) and adds it to the residual stream as it generates. Tune it live in the console; it is steering, never a prompt.

Research. On the Research tab, Renji guards a topic through the conscience, then pushes your model through six passes (Survey → Cluster → Gap → Hypothesis → Inversion → Synthesis) and writes the finding as a PDF document. Renji directs; the model does the research.

Privacy & limits

  • Your model and weights never leave your machine; the heart only sees the turn's text.
  • This is for testing during the event — an open trial.
  • Nothing here exposes the heart's internals. By design.

This is only a part of Renji

What you're running is a faithful slice. The full system is not published, on purpose, for two reasons:

  • It's still being built. What you see is real and runs today, but it's part of a larger system under active development.
  • Some of what the full system can do is not safe to hand out. It's powerful enough that releasing all of it openly would be dangerous — which is exactly the point of the part you can see: Renji exists to keep capability good. We hold the rest back until that holds under every condition.
